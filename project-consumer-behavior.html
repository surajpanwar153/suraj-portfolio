Consumer Behavior & Social Media-Driven Shopping

An analytical study on how social media influences purchasing decisions, trust, and spending behavior among college students.

Back to Projects

Business Context

Social media platforms have become major drivers of consumer discovery, influence, and purchasing behavior. For brands targeting younger consumers, understanding how trust, persuasion, and social proof operate in digital environments is critical for marketing effectiveness.

Problem Statement

The project aimed to understand how social media affects purchasing decisions among college students, identify the key psychological and behavioral factors influencing trust, and examine differences between traditional and social media-driven shopping behavior.

Approach & Methodology

  • Designed a structured online survey using Google Forms
  • Target sample size of 150–200 college students
  • Applied a mixed-methods approach combining quantitative and experimental elements
  • Integrated concepts from consumer psychology and behavioral economics
  • Analyzed patterns in trust, influence, and spending behavior

Tools Used

  • Google Forms
  • Excel / Google Sheets
  • Basic statistical analysis
  • Data visualization techniques

Key Insights

  • Social proof and influencer credibility significantly affect purchase intent
  • Emotional triggers often outweigh rational evaluation in social media purchases
  • Trust is shaped more by peer validation than by brand messaging
  • Spending behavior differs noticeably between traditional and digital channels

Business Implications

The findings highlight the importance of trust-building mechanisms, authentic influencer strategies, and emotionally resonant content for brands operating in digital channels. Businesses can use these insights to design more effective marketing strategies and improve customer engagement.
